Software Engineer Intern

YouTube

Duration May 2025 - August 2025
Location San Bruno, CA
Team YouTube Music

Overview

At YouTube, I worked on the YouTube Music team developing features for the iOS and Android apps. This role significantly expanded my mobile development skills and taught me the importance of working at scale— every feature I touched would be used by tens of millions of listeners worldwide. The experience gave me perspective on production engineering, accessibility, and collaborative development in a fast-paced environment.

Impact Projects

In-App Lyrics Feature (Full-Stack)

Developed a full-stack feature for YouTube Music's in-app lyrics on Android and iOS, handling the entire implementation pipeline. The feature was successfully launched to production and is used by tens of millions of listeners worldwide. This was my primary project and represents significant scope for an internship.

Critical Bug Fix: Text Direction Accessibility

Identified and resolved a critical yearlong bug by implementing a lyrics text-direction fix. This improved accessibility and readability across different song and app languages for a global user base, preventing display issues for non-Latin scripts.

Component Modernization

Modernized core components of the YouTube Music app, addressing client feedback and elevating product quality at scale. Focused on accessibility improvements and ensuring consistency across platforms.

Animation Framework Contribution

Engineered a complex transition animation using YouTube's low-level internal language with minimal documentation. Identified and fixed a critical animation framework bug that affected all developers, improving reliability platform-wide and receiving positive feedback from senior engineers.

Beyond-Scope Initiative

Collaborated with full-time engineers to integrate a critical pipeline and proactively implemented additional high-impact tasks beyond assigned scope, helping accelerate overall project delivery and demonstrating strong ownership.

Key Takeaways

  • Scale Changes Everything: Building for millions of users requires attention to edge cases, accessibility, and internationalization in ways that smaller projects don't.
  • Proactive Problem-Solving: Looking for bugs beyond my assignment and contributing beyond scope made a real difference in the team's success and earned respect from senior engineers.
  • Accessibility is Not Optional: The text-direction bug taught me how quickly accessibility issues can go unnoticed but have significant impact on global users.
  • Documentation Matters: Working with poorly documented internal systems taught me to appreciate good documentation and write clear docs myself.
  • Shipping is the Hard Part: Getting a feature from development through testing, review, and production launch involves many stakeholders and careful coordination.
